Ada Lovelace

"First" computer programmer, Daughter of Lord Bryon

Watercolor Portrait of Ada Lovelace

Timeline

  • 1815 - Born in London
  • 1823 - Father (Lord Bryon) dies in Greece
  • 1833 - Met Charles Babbage
  • 1835 - Married William King
  • 1838 - William King inherits noble title which makes Ada Countess of Lovelace
  • 1843 - Babbage enlists Ada to publish translation of the Analytical Engine adding her own notes
  • 1852 - Death by uterine cancer. She was burried beside her father

Information

  • Her father, Lord Bryon, is a renown poet. Some of his poems were about her. However, she never met him.
  • Her mother was proficient in Math. Bryon called her the "Princess of Parallelograms". She prevented Ada from feeling any trace of him
  • Ada wrote a book called Flyology on her interest in mechanical engineering
  • She was influenced by her father to create "poetical science".
  • She died at the same age of her father (36 years old).
  • She is considered the "first computer programmer" as she introduced the first concepts of computers.
  • A program language "Ada" was named after her.
  • Her notes on the Analytical Engine influenced Alan Turing's work on modern computers.
